Case study · outside Shanghai, China · 2012

Shanghai, China: the 2012 base-stabilization engagement

A street base outside Shanghai, graded and stabilized with the product and cement in 2012, with a written demonstration specification prepared for the City of Shanghai. The record shows the base work and the spec; it does not show a finished street, and this page says so.

Field record  Outcome undocumented   Sources: SSPMB photo deck; City of Shanghai PMB Road Renewal Demo specification
What the record shows

A graded street and a written spec

The photo record is two frames from 2012: a delegation inspecting the graded, unpaved street base, and the full stabilized base running through a tree-lined commercial street, captioned in the source deck as "Base Stabilization outside Shanghai, China 2012" and "China street base in conjunction with SSPMB and cement."

Alongside the photos, a demonstration work specification survives, written for the City of Shanghai PMB Road Renewal Demo: pulverize the existing base to a minimum 5 inches, apply PMB at 0.65 gal/SY at 4:1 water dilution to 5–8% optimum moisture, surface with emulsion coats and a hot-mix wear course, Wirtgen 2400 / Cat RM300-class recycler, minimum 50°F, four-hour traffic closure.

  • 2012, street base outside Shanghai; product used together with cement
  • Demo spec: pulverize to 5" minimum; 0.65 gal/SY PMB; water:PMB 4:1; emulsion + HMA wear course
  • Spec's dosage math: 0.65 gal/SY over 5" ≈ 0.13 gal/SY per inch, consistent with the field-derived figure tracked in the dosage question
Read before citing this study.
  • No finished-street photo exists anywhere in the archive, both photographs show graded base, not a completed pavement. A later deck re-captioned these same photos "Street Paving in Shanghai"; the honest caption is base stabilization.
  • The City of Shanghai demo specification is a proposal/scope document: no client signature, no built quantities, no test results, and no confirmation the demo proceeded is on record.
  • No Chinese client, district, or counterpart is named anywhere in the record. Whether the demo was built is on the manufacturer question list.
  • The base was stabilized with the product and cement together, the same attribution caveat as the Nicaragua and rammed-earth records.
